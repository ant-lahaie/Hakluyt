A voyage of certaine English men under the  conduct of Lewes king of France unto the Holy land.{n}1147.{n}Tempore regis Stephani{n}The same in English.{n}BOth the princes provision being made for so great an expedition, they severing their armies, entered on their journey. For the Emperour Conradus went before, certaine dayes journey, with very great power of Italians, Germans, and other countreys. And king Lewes followed after accompanied with a band of horsemen and footmen of French men, Flemmings, Normans, Britons, English men, Burgundions, men of ; Provence, and Gascoins.{n}.
