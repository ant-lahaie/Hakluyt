a letter of the english ambassador to m. Edward Barton . 
 after Barton i send you 3. commandments in turkish , with a copy thereof in english , to the end our ship may not come in danger of breach of league , if they shall shoot at the galley of those of Algiers , Tunis , and Tripoli in the west : which after you 
 have show the bassa , receive again into your hand , and see they register , and then deliver one of they to our friend m. Tipton , and the like you be to do with the privilege which you carry with you , and see they joint register in the cadies book , deliver the copy of the say privilege seal by the Cadi , also to the say our friend m. Tipton , take a note of his hand for the receipt thereof , and for delivery at all time to we or our assign . and require they in her majesty and the grand signors name , that they will have our ship pass too and fro under licence and safeconduct for recommend in friendly manner . touch your proceed in Tripoli with Romadan , as i have not receive any advise thereof , since your departure , so must i leave you to God and my former direction . the ship patronise of Hassan ray , which you write to be we , prove to be a catalonian . as for we , by report of that Hassan and other Jew in his ship , it be affirm to be sell to the Malteses , which with the rest you be to receive there . and have end these affair and register our privilege , and these three commandments , in Tripoli , Tunis , and Alger , i pray you make 274 
 speedy return , and for that which may be recover , make over the same either to Richard row for Patrasso in Morea , or otherwise hither to John bear in the sure manner you may , if the register of that your privilege and these commandment will not suffer you in person to return with the same . from my mansion Rapamat in Pera this 24. of June 1584.
