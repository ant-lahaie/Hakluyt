the voyage of Henry earl of Derbie , after duke of Hereford , and last king of England , by the name of Henry the four , An. Dom. 1390. into Prussia and Lettowe , against the infidel , record by Thomas of Walsingham . 
 a about the same time lord Henry the earl of Derbie travail into Prussia , where , with the help of the marshal of the same province , and of a certain king call Wytot , he vanquish the army of the king of Lettowe , with the captivity of four lithuanian duke , and the slaughter of three , beside more than three hundred of the principali common soldier of the say army which be slay . the city also which be call Will or Vilna , into the castle whereof the king of Lettow name Skirgalle flee for his safeguard , be , by the valour of the say earl especial and of his follower , surprise and take . for certain of the chief man of his family , while other be slothful or at least ignorant of their intent , scale the wall , advance his colour thereupon . and there be take and slay four thousand of the common soldier , and among other be slay the king of Poland his brother , who be our profess enemy . and the castle of the foresay city be besiege for the space of five week : but by reason of the infirmity and inconvenience wherewith the whole army be annoy , the great master of Prussia and of Lifland will not stay any long . there be convert of the nation of Lettowe eight person unto the christian faith . and the master of Lifland carry home with he into his country three thousand captive .
