There was a mill above the town , and upon the top of another hill in the woods stood a little watchhouse , where we took twentie sows of silver , two bars of gold , some money in coin , beside other pillage . The town was situated in a watery soil , and subject much to rain , very unhealthy as any place in the Indies , having great store of Oranges , plantans , cassavy - roots , & such other fruits ; but very dangerous to be eaten for breeding of diseases . To the Eastward of the town within the bay runneth out a fresh river of excellent good water , with houses , and all about it gardens : half a league from hence due East into the country was and Indian town , whither as we marched a little before our coming away with and hundred men they had broken down a bridge to hinder our passage , where they lay in ambush with some twentie or thirtie small shot , and bows and arrows , set upon us , and killed Lieutenant Jones , hurt three or foure and so fled into the woods , ran before us and fired their own town , and then fled farther into the woods : our men fired diverse other houses in pursuing them , and so returned again : our general with Sir Thomas being in the Rivers mouth with thirtie or fortie men filling water about some mile from us .
