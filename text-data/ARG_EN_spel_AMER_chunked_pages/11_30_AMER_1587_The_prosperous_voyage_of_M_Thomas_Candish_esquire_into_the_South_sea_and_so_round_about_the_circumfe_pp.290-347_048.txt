Trade from Acapulco to Manilla . 329 Silver exchanged weight for weight for gold . The island of Capul at which our men stayed 9 days . One of the chiefs governors of the island came aboard us . Espanna , and also 20 or 30 ships from China and from the Sanguelos , which bring them many sorts of merchandise . The merchants of China and the Sanguelos are part Moors and part heathen people . They bring great store of gold with them , which they traffic and exchange for silver , and give weight for weight . These Sanguelos are men of marvelous capacity in devising and making all manner of things , especially in all handy crafts and sciences : and every one is so expert , perfect , and skilful in his faculty , as few or no Christians are able to go beyond them in that which they take in hand . For drawing & embroidering upon satin , silck , or lawn , either beast , fowl , fish or worm , for liveliness and perfectness , both in silk , silver , gold , & pearl , they excel . Also the 14 day at night we entered the straights between the island of Luzon , & the island of Camlaia . The fifteenth of January we fell with and island called Capul , and had betwixt the said island and another island but and narrow passage , and a marvelous rippling of a very great tIED with a ledge of rocks lying off the point of the island of Capul : and no danger but water enough a fair breadth off : and within the point a fair bay and a very good harbour in foure fathoms water hard aboard the shore within a cables length .
