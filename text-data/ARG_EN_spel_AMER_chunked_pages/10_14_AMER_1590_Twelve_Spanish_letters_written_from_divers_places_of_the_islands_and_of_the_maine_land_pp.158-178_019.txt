The canoas are drawn up this river of Magdalena by main force of the mariners in rowing and haling them with 175 ropes . There are 7 . or 8 . Indians commonly which guide these canoas , beside the Master which keepeth the helm , and the passengers . We are commonly two months in going up this river . It is 150 . leagues to the landing place . And there the merchants unladen their merchandise , which serve for all the cities and towns which are in this new kingdom e of Granada . And the merchants lade the canoas back again from thence with great store of silver and gold which is got out of the mines for Cartagena , and there it is shipped for Spaine . And likewise here is great quantity of treasure laden in the said canoas which is for the kings custom and other duties which are paid : But they are but a month or three weeks going down the river to Cartagena . These are the richest mines in all Peru . And thus I rest . From Santa Fee de Bogota in the new kingdom of Granada in Peru the 10 . of May 1590 . The Licentiate John de Labera .
