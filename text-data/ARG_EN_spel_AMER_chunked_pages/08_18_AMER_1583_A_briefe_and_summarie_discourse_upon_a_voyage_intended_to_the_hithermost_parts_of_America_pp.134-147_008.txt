I speak not this by the Merchants whom for their freedoms of trade 1 would not have pressed to any further charge then this first preparation , but rather by such as have great affection to hazard the changing of their estates , and would be well content to go in the voyage if they might only be assured that there is such a Country , & that their money should not be wasted to nothing in the preparations . The right examination of this point must be the contrary sequel of the common Proverb that is used , Nothing venture , nothing have : so on the other side by venturing , many great good profits are found out , to the wonderful benefit of the Common weal , and to those especially in private , who take on them the hazard of their life and travel , or substance in the first attempts : and therefore I would wish that they , who ( God be thanked ) are well able to spare that which is required of each one towards the undertaking of this adventure , be well content and willing to employ the same , since the sequel in good and substantiali reason does promise , not only a great commodity in particular to the Merchant , who shall here at home exercise the trade of Merchandise : but also to and infinite number of other , who presently live in poor estate , and may by taking the opportunity of this discovery , altar the same to a far better degree .
