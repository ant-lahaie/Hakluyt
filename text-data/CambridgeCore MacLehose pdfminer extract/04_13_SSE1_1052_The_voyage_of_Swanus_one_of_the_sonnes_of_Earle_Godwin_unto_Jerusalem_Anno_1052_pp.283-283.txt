The voyage of Swanus one of the sonnes of Earle Godwin unto Jerusalem, Anno Dom. 1052, recorded by William of Malmsburie lib. 2. de gestis regum Anglorum, Capite 13. fWanus perversi ingenii & infidi in fegem, multoties a patre & fratre Haroldo descivit : & pirata factus, praedis maritimis virtutes majorum polluit. Postremi pro conscientia Brunonis cognati interempti, & (ut quidam dicunt) fratris Jerosolimam abiit : indeque rediens, a Saracenis circumventus, & ad mortem csesus est. T he same in English. [II. i. 7] lesse to the king, often SWanus being of a perverse disposition, and faithtimes disagreed with his father and his brother Harold : and afterwards prooving a pirate, he stained the vertues of his ancestours with his robberies upon the seas. Last of all, being guilty unto himselfe of the murther of his kinseman Bruno, and (as some do report) of his owne brother, he in his returne home, travailed unto Jerusalem : and being taken by the Saracens, was beaten, and wounded unto death. 283
