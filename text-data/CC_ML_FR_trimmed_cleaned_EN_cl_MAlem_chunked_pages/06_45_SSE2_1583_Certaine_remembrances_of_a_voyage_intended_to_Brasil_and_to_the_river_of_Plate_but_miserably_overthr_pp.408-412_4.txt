Rio grande . one Coxe a old english man bury alive by the moor of Rio grande in Guinea . he say , that the 17 day of July , anno 1583 . have some lack of fresh water , they put room upon the coast of guinie , where they be set upon a sand about 8 league from the shore , and this examinate , with 29 more , get into the pinnace , who arrive in a island , be desolate of people , and five mile in compass , where they rest 18 day through force of weather , have nought to eat but grass . the rest of the company the ship be split in two , and in quarter , get they into one of they after quarter , and by the help of raft come also a shore into another island near to Rio grande , where they all die as he suppose . the other 30 in the pinnace , at the end of 18 day , depart that island , and come to saint Domingo , where come on shore , they be take of the moor , and strip naked . and they bury one Coxe a old man alive , notwithstanding his pitiful lamentation and skriking : the rest have rice and water allow they , live there a certain time . this examinate be at last sell to a Portugal , with who he dwell the space of a quarter of a year , and in the end , a Portugal caravel come thither , his master jade the same with negro , and he obtain leave of his master to go in the same caravel , and by that mean arrive at Lisbon , and from thence come into England the 17 of October , 1584 , leave behind he of his company alive , Richard Hacker , John Baker , John Matthew , and a boy , with two other which be go beyond saint Domingo : all which , as he say , be so sick and disease , that he judge they to be long before this time dead .
