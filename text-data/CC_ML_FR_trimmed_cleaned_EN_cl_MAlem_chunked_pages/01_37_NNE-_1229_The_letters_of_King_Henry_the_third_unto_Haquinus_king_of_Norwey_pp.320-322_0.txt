320 the letter of king Henry the three unto Haquinus king of Norway concern a treaty of peace and mutual traffic of merchandise , etc HEnry by the grace of God , etc unto Haquinus by the same grace king of Norway , send greeting . we render unto your highness unspeakable thank for those thing which by your letter , and by your discreet subject the abbot of Lisa , you have signify unto we , and also for that you be right willing and desirous to begin and to conclude between we both , a league of peace and amity . and we for our part both now be , and hereafter shall well content , that both our land be common , to the end that the merchant and people of your dominion may free and without impediment resort unto our land , and our people and merchant may likewise have recourse unto your territory . provide , that for the confirmation of this matter , you send unto we your letter patent , and we will send we also unto you . howbeit in the mean while we do will and free grant , that the merchant both of our and your land , may go , come , and return to and from both our dominion . and if there be aught in your mind , whereby we may stand you in any stead , you may bold signify the same unto we . we have as yet detain the foresay abbot , that we may , to our ability , cause restitution to be make for your ship , and for the thing therein contain : by who we will certify you of our own estate , and of the estate of our 1 321 ten kingdom so soon , etc witness our self at lamhith the ten of October . another letter in the same form and to the same effect be there and then send unto S . duke of Norway . the same in english .
