Newberie , write to master Leonard poor of London , from Aleppo . right well - beloved , my very hearty commendation unto you , and the rest of my friend remember . my last i send you be the 25 . of February last , from deal out of the down , after which time with contrary wind we remain upon our own coast , until the 11 . day of march , and then we set sail from march u . Falmouth , and the thirteenth day the wind come contrary with a very great storm , which continue eight day , and in this great storm we have some of our good wet , but God be thank no great hurt do . after which time we sail with a fair wind within the strait , and so remain at sea , and anchor at no 453 amber grief . Sope . break glass . place until our come into the road of Tripoli in Syria , which be the last day of April . this be a very good passage . God make we thankful for it . the fourteen day of this present we come from Tripoli , and the twenty day arrive here in Aleppo , and with the help of God to morrow or next day , we begin our voyage towards Babylon and Balsara , and so into India . our friend master Barret have he commend to you , who have send you in the Emanuel a ball of nutmeg for the small trifle you send he , which i hope long since you have receive . also he have by his letter certify you in what order he sell those thing , whereof i can say nothing , because i have not see the account thereof , neither have demand it : for ever since our come hither he have be still busy about the dispatch of the ship , and our voyage , and i likewise in buy of thing here to carry to Balsara , and the Indies . we have buy in coral for 1200 . and odd ducat , and amber for four hundred ducat , and some soap and break glass , with all other small trifle , all which thing i hope will serve very well for those place that we shall go unto . all the rest of the account of the bark Reinolds be send home in the Emanuel , which be 3600 . ducat , which be 200 .
