the voyage pass by sea into Aegypt , by John [ ii i . 281 . ] Evesham gentleman . anno 1586 . he 5 of December 1586 we depart from Gravesend in the tiger of London , wherein be master under God for the voyage Robert Rickman , and the 21 . day at night we come to the isle of wight : depart from thence in the morning follow we have a fair wind , so that on the 27 day we come in sight of the rock of Lisbon , and so sail along we come in sight of the south cape , the 29 of the same , and on the morrow with a westerly wind we enter the straight : and the second of January be as high as cape de gate , we depart from our fleet towards Argier . and the 4 day we arrive at the port of Argier aforesaid , where we stay till the first of march . at which time we set sail towards a place call Tunis , to the eastward of Argier 100 league , where we arrive the 8 of the same . This Tunis be a Tunis . small city up 12 mile from the sea , and at the port or ride where ship doe ride , be a castle or fort call Goletta , sometime in the hand of the christian , but now of the Turk : at which place we remain till the three of April : at which time we set sail towards Alexandria , and have sometime fair wind , sometime contrary , we pass on the 12 day between Sicilia and Malta ( where near adjoin have be the fort and hold of the knight of the Rhodes ) and so the 19 day we fall with the isle of Candy , and from thence to Alexandria , where we arrive the 27 of April , and there continue till the 5 of October . the say city of Alexandria be a old thing decay or the descrip - ruinated , have be a fair and great city near two fan ofalex - mile in length , be all vaunt underneath for provision andria . of fresh water , which water come thither but once every year , out of one of the four river of paradise ( as it be term ) call Nilus , which in September flow near eighteen foot upright high than his accustom manner
