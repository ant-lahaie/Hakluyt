Richard Hakluit , concern the first ambassage to our most gracious queen Elizabeth from the russian emperor anno 1567 , and other notable matter incident to those place and time . orshipfull sir , because i find you have the success and proceed of Osep Napea the first ambassador of the russian emperor to the majesty of king Philip and queen marry , at what time and at his return i be remain in Russia , and do not find that the perfect knowledge of the first ambassage from thence to this our sovereign lady queen Elizabeth be come to your hand , between who highness and the ambassador i be interpretor , i think good to express it . in August anno 1567 arrive at London with their retinue two especial authorise messenger , name Stephen Twerdico , and Theodore Pogorella , with letter and present to her majesty , at that time be at otelands , where diverse of the chief merchant of the russian company do associate they , and i there do my duty and office of interpretor , her majesty give they audience . first they rehearse the long stile and majesty of their master , with his most friendly and hearty commendation to her highness , and then they testify the singular great joy and pleasure that he conceive to hear of her most princely estate , dignity and health : and last , they deliver their letter and present . the present send unto her majesty be sable , both in pair for tippet , and two timbar , to wit , two time forty , with lusern and other rich fur . for at that time that princely ancient ornament of fur be yet in use . and great pity but that it may be renew , especial in court , and among magistrate , not 98 only for the restore of a old worshipful art and company , but also because they be for our climate 7 % use of wholesome , delicate , grave and comely : express dig - f ^ e * micate nitie , comfort age , and of
