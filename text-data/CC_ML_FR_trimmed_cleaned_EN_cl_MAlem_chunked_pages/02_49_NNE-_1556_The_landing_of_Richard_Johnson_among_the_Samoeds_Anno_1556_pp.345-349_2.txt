Pechere , which be in subjection to the emperor of Russia , when they will remove from one place to another , than they will make sacrifice in manner follow . every kindred do sacrifice in their own tent , and he that be most ancient be their priest . and first the priest do begin to play upon a thing like to a great sieve , with a skin on the one end like a drum : and the stick that he play with be about a spann e long , and one end be round like a ball , cover with the skin of a heart . also the priest have upon his head a thing of white like a garland , and his face be cover with a piece of a shirt of mail , with many small rib , and tooth of fish , and wild beast hang on the same mail . than he sing as we use here in England to hallow , whope , or shout at hound , and the rest of the company answer he with this Owtis , Igha , Igha , Igha , and then the priest repli again with his voice . and they answer he with the selfsame word so many time , that in the end he become as it be mad , and fall down as he be dead , have nothing on he but a shirt , lie upon his back i may perceive he to breathe . i ask they why he lie so , and they answer i , now do our god tell he what we shall do , and whither we shall go . and when he have lie still a little while , they cry thus three time together , Oghao , Oghao , Oghao , and as they use these three call , he rise with his head and lie down again , and then he rise up and sing with like voice as he do before : and his audience answer he , Igha , Igha , Igha . than he command they to kill five olens or great deer , and continue sing still both he and they as before . than he take a sword of a cubit and a span long , ( i do mete it my self ) and put it into his belly halfway and sometime less , but no wound be to be see , 347 A . D . 1556 .
