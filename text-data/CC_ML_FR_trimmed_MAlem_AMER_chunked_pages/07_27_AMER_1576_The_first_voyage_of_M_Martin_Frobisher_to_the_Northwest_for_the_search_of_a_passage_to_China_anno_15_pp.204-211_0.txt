the first voyage of m . Martine Frobisher , to the northwest , for the search of the straight or passage to China , write by Christopher hall , master in the Gabriel , and make in the year of our lord 1576 . he 7 . of June be Thursday , the two bark , viz . the Gabriel , and the two Michael and our pinnace set sail at Ratcliff , and bear down to Detford , and there we anchor : the cause be , that our pinnace burst her boultsprit , and foremast aboard of a ship that ride at Detford , else we mean to have pass that day by the court then at Grenewich . the 8 . day be Friday , about 12 of the clock we weigh at Detford , and set sail all three of we , and bear down by the court , where we shoot off our ordinance and make the best show we can : her majesty behold the same , commend it , and bid we farewell , with shake her hand at we out of the window . afterwards she send a gentleman aboard of we , who declare that her majesty have good like of our do , and thank we for it , and also will our captain to come the next day to the court to take his leave of she . the same day towards night m . secretary Woolly come aboard of we , and declare to the company , that her majesty have appoint he to give they charge to be obedient , and diligent to their captain , and governor in all thing , and wish we happy success . the 12 . day be over against Gravesend , by the castle or blockhouse , we observe the latitude , which be 51 . degree 33 . min . and in that place the variation of the compass be 11 . degree and a half . fair island . the 24 . day at 2 . of the clock after noon , i have sight of fair isle , be from we 6 . league north and by east , and when i bring it northwest and by north , 204 it do rise at the
