Gibbes master , Christopher hall pilot , accompany with the rear - admiral name the hopewel , who captain be m . Henry carewe , the m . Andrew Dier , and certain other ship come to Gravesend , where we anchor and abide the come of certain of our fleet which be not yet come . the 27 . of the same month our fleet be now come together , and all thing press in a readiness , the wind favour , and tide serve , we be of sail in number eight , weigh anchor and hoist our sail towards Harwich to meet with our admiral , and the residue which then and there abide our arrival : where we safe arrive the 28 . thereof , find there our admiral , who we with the discharge of certain piece salute , accord to order and duty , and be welcome with the like courtesy : which be finish we land : where our general continue mustering his soldier and miner , and set thing in order appertain to the voyage until the last of the say month of May , which day we hoist our sail , and commit our self to the conduct of almighty God , we set forward towards the west country in such lucky wise and good success , that by the five of June we pass the dursy , be the utmost part of Ireland to the westward . and here it be not much amiss nor far from our purpose , if i shall a little discourse and speak of our adventure and chance by the way , as our land at Plymouth , as also the meeting certain poor man , which be rob and spoil of all that they have by 231 master
