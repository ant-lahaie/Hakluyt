For the Squid , whose nature is to come by night as well as by day , I tell them , I set him a candle to see his way , with which he is much delighted , or else comes to wonder at it as doe our fresh water fish , the other comes also in the night , but chiefly in the day , being forced by the Cod that would devour him , and therefore for fear coming so near the shore , is driven dry by the surge of the Sea on the pebble and sands . Of these being as good as a Smelled you may take up with a shove - net as plentifully as you do Wheat in a shovel , sufficient in three or foure hours for a whole City . There be also other fish which I tell those that are desirous of strange news , that I take as fast as one would gather up stones , and them I take with a long pole and hook . Yea marry say they , we believe so , and that you catch all the rest you bring home in that sort , from Portugals and Frenchmen . No surely , but thus I do : with three hooks stretched forth in the end of a pole , I make as it were and Eel spear , with which I prick those Flounders as fast as you would take up fritters with a sharp pointed stick , and with that tool I may take up in less then half a day Lobsters sufficient to find three hundred men for a days meat .
