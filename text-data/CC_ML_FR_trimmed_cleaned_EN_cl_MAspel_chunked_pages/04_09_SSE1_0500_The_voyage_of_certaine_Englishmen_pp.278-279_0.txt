Certain Englishmen sent to Constantinople by the French King to Justinian the Emperor , about the year of Christ , 500 . out of the fourth book of Procopius de Bello Gothico . The same in English . THe Isle of Britaine is inhabited by three most populous nations , every of which is governed by a several king . The said nations are nam Angili , Frisones , and Britones , which last are called after the name of the Island . In this Isle there are such swarms of people , that every year they go forth in great numbers with their wives and children into France . And the Frenchmen right willingly receive them into their land , which seems very desolate for want of inhabitants . Whereupon it is said that the French do challenge the foresaid Island unto themselves . For not long since , when the king of the Frankes sent certain of his subject ' s ambassadors to Constantinople unto Justinian the Emperor , he sent English men also , am - 278 bitiously boasting , as though the said Isle had be under his jurisdiction .
