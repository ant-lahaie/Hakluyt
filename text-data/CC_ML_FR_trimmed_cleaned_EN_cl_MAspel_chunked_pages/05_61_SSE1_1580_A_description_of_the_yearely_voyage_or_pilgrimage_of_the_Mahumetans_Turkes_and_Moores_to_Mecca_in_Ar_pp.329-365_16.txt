The last night before their departure they make great feasting and triumph within the Carovan , with castles and other infinite devises of firework , the Janizaries always standing round about the tent of the Captain with such shouting and joy , that on every side the earth resoundeth , and this night they discharge all their ordinance , foure or sixe times , and after at the break of the day upon the found of a trumpet they march forward on their way . What times the Carovan travelleth , and when it rests . IT is to be noted , that from Cairo to Mecca they make 40 days journey or thereabouts , & the same great days journeys . For the custom of the Carovan is to travel much and rest little , and ordinarily they journey in this manner : They travel from two a clock in the morning until the sun rising , then having rested till noon , they set forward , and so continue till night , & then also rest again , as is abovesaid , till two of the clock : and this order they observe until the end of the voyage , never changing the same , except in some places , whereof we will hereafter speak , where for respect of water they rest sometime a day and and half , and this they observe to refresh themselves , otherwise both man and beast would die . In what order the Carovan travelleth . THe manner and order which the Carovan observeth in marching is this . It goes divided into three parts , to wit , the forward , the main battle , and the rearward . In the forward go the 8 Pilots before with a Chaus , which hath foure knaves , & each knave carrieth a sinew of a bull , to the end that if occasion requires , you bastonado 344 may be given to such as deserve the same . These knaves cast offenders down , turning up the soles of their feet made fast to a staff
