339 The letters of Edward the second unto Haquinus king of Norway , concerning the English merchants arrested in Norway , and their goods to be freed from arrest . O the mighty Prince , lord Haquinus , by the grace of God the famous king of Norway his most deer friend , Edward by the same grace of God , king of England , lord of Ireland , duke of Aquitaine , greeting and sincere love . We marvel not a little , and are much dis - quieted in our cogitations , considering the grievances and oppressions , which ( as we have been informed by pitiful complaints ) are at this present , more then in times past , without any reasonable cause inflicted upon our subject ' s , which do usually resort unto your kingdom for traffics sake .
