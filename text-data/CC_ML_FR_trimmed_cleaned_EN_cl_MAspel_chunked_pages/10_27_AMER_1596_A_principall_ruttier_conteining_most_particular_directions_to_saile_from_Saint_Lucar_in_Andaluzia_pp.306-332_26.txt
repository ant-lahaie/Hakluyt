Likewise from thence on the side of Havana you shall see certain hills which show to be three , and lie Northeast and Southwest , and that on the Southwest is highest : they are called Las Sierras de Guana : and North and South from them lies a flat , which is called Cayo de Nicola , which stretched out two leagues into the Sea , and in the middle of the said flat there is a little Island of white sand : and from thence you must sail to Cruz deal Padre . Marks of Cruz deal Padre . CRuz del Padre is a Cape all broken in pieces : and from thence you must stir away Northeast , because of the Bay that is there , called the Bay of Conell , and this Bay stretcheth to the Bay of Caos , which is within 20 leagues of the said point : and then stir away West Northwest to go with the Port of Matanzas , and with the hills of Camoniaca . Marks of the hills of Camoniaca . THe hills of Camoniaca lie Northwest and Southeast , and the Southeast side is low land and even : also upon the Northeast side it is even land , and runneth towards the Bay of Matanzas , and in the middle of these hills there is one high hill , and upon the Northeast side there appears a round heap : and 330 if you see this hill at West Southwest , the Pan or round heap of Matanzas will bear off you West and by South .
