The passport made Malta unto the Raynolds . 1582. 
 by the great Master of Englishmen in the bark 
 Rear Hugo de Loubeux Verdala , Dei gratia sacrae domus hospitalis sancti Joannis Hierosolymitani magister humilis , pauperumque Jesu Christi custos , universis & singulis principibus ecclesiasticis & secularibus , archiepiscopis , episcopis , ducibus , marchionibus , baronibus , no

 Notum facimus & in verbo veritatis attestamur , come 
 nel mese di Maggio prossime passato le nostre galere vennero dal viaggio di Barberia , dove havendo mandato per soccorrere a un galionetto de Christiani che havea dato traverso in quelle parti , essendo arrivati sopra questa isola alia parte de ponente trovarono una nave Inglesa , sopra cargo de essa il magnifico Giovanni Keale , & David Filly patrono , volendo la reconoscere i nave fosse , han visto , che se metteva in ordine per defenders ! , dubitando che debt nostre galere fossero de inimici : & per che un marinaro rivoltose contra la volonta de detti magnifico Giovani Keale & David Filly , habbi tirato un tiro di artiglieria verso una de debt galere , & i non se amangnava la vela de la Majestra secondo la volonta de detti magnifico Giovanni Keale & David Filly patrono , furimensata detta nave nel present general porto di Malta , secondo 1’ordine dei venerando Generale de debt galere , & essendo qua , monsignor Inquisitore would impedita quella per conto dei sancto officio , & si died parte alia santita di nostro

Han donque humilmente supplicato detti magnifico Giovanni Keale & David Filly per nome & parte delli magnifici Edwardo Osborn senator & Richardo Staper merchant ! Inglesi della nobile citta di Londra , & anco di Thomaso Wilkinson scrivano , piloti , nocheri , & marinari , gli volessimo dare le nostre lettere patent & salvo condutto , accioche potranno andare & ritornare quando gli parera commodo con alcuna roba & mercantia a loro benvista : si come noi , essendo cosa giusta & i retornera commoda a nostra relligione & a question forrestieri , per tenor de li presenti se gli habiamo concesse con le condition pero infra scritte . videlicet : 
 Che ogni volta che detti mercadanti con so nave o con altra non porteranno mercantie de contrabando , & i constara per fede authentica & con lettere patent de sanita , poteran liberalmente victualiarse de tutte le victuarie necessary , & praticare in questa isola & dominii , & poi partirsene & seguire suo viaggio per dove volessero in levante o altrove , come tutti altri vaselli & specialmente de Frances ! & altri nationi , & di vendere & comprare qual si voglia mercantia a loro benvista . 
 Item , i potera portare polvere de canon & di archibuso , salnitro , carboni di petra rosetta , platine de rame , stagno , acciale , ferro , caris6e commune , tela grossa bianca per far tend de galere , ball de ferro de calibro , petre de molino fine , arbore & antenne de galere , bas-tardi & alteri . Et in conclusion , havenda visto che loro per il tempo che restarano qua , si portorno da fideli & Catholici Christiani , & i sua sanctita habbia trovato bono il salvo condutto del gran Turko a loro concesso , per il timor della armata Turkesca & di altri vaselli de inimici , inherendo alia volonta di sua sanctita , & massime per che havera de andare & passare per diversi lochi & tanto lontani come Ingilterra , Flandra , & tutti parti di ponente , 8c in altrove , a noi would parson farle le present nostre lettere patent come fidele conversatore nostro , 216 
 accio piu securamente & sensa obstaculo possa andare & ritornare quando li parera con detta nave oh con altre , a loro benvista . Per tanto donque tutti & ciascun di voi sudetti affectuosamente pregamo , che per qual si voglia de vostra jurisditione , alia quale detto magnifico Giovani Keale & David Filly a nome quo supra con la nave & marinari de detti loro principali o altri caschera , navigare , passare , & venire sicuramente , alia libera , sensa alcuno disturbo o altro impedimento li lasciate , & facciate lasciare stare , & passare , tornare , & quando li parera partire , talmente che per amore & contemplation nostra il detto magnifico Giovanni Keale a nome quo supra con le nave , marinari , & mercantia non habbi difficult , fastidio & ritentione alcuna , anzi se gli dia ogni agiuto & favore , cosa degnadi voi , giusta , 8c a noi gratissima , de recom-pensarvila con uguale 8c maggior servitio , quando dall’occasione ne saremo rechiesti . Et finalmente com-mandammo a tutti 8c qual si voglia relligiosi 8c ffati de nostra relligione di qual si voglia condition , grado 8c stato che siano , 8c a tutti ricevitori 8c procuratori nostri in tutti 8c qual si voglia priorati nostri deputati 8c deputandi in virtue di santa obedientia , 8c a tutti nostri vassalli 8c alia giurisditione di nostra relligione sogetti , i in tale 8c per tale tenghino 8c reputino il detto magnifico Giovanni Keale a nome ut supra , nave , marinari , 8c mercantia , sensa permittere , i nel detto suo viaggio , o in alcun altro luogo sia molestato , o in qual si voglia manera impedito , anzi tutte le cose sue 8c negotii loro sian da voi agioutati 8c continuamente favoriti . In cujus rei testimonium Bulla nostra magistralis in cera nigra praesentibus est impressam . Datae Melitae in conventu nostro die duodecimo Mensis Julii . 1582.

 FRier Hugo of Loubeux Verdala , by the grace of God , master of the holy house , the hospital of S. 
 John at Jerusalem , and and humble keeper of the poor of Jesus Christ , to all 8c every prince ecclesiastical 8c secular , 217 
 archbishop's , bishops , Dukes , Marquesses , Barons , Captains , Vicelords , Majors , Castellans , Admirals , and whatsoever patrons of Galleys , or other greater ships , and governors of cities , potentates and magistrates , and other officers and persons whatsoever , of what dignity , degree , state and condition soever they be , dwelling in all places and lands , greeting . 
 We make it known , and in the word of truth do witness , that in the month of May last past , our galleys came on the voyage from Barbarie , where having commandment to succour a little ship of the Christians which was driven over into that part , being arrived upon this Hand on the West part they found one English ship under the charge of the worshipful John Keel , and David Filly master : and our men willing to know what ship it was , they seemed to put themselves in order for their defence , doubting that the said our galleys were of the enemies , & therefore one mariner attempted contrary to the will of the worshipful John Keel , and David Filly master , and had shot off a piece of artillery against one of the said galleys , and because she would not strike amain her sail , according to the will of the said worshipful John Keel , and David Filly master , the said ship was brought back again unto the present port of Malta , according to the order of the reverend general of the said galleys : and in being there , master Inquisitor stayed it by authority of the holy office , and in that behalf by the holy ness of our Lord pope Gregorie the thirteenth , in the end was licenced to depart on her voyage . They therefore the said worshipful John Keel and David Filly , in the name and behalf of the worshipful master Edward Osborne Aiderman , and Richard Staper , English merchants of the noble city of London , have humbly besought together with Thomas Wilkinson the purser , pilots , master and mariners , that we would give our letters patents , and safe conducts , that they might go and return , when they shall see opportunity , with their goods and merchandizes at their pleasure : whereupon the

 A PASSPORT TO ENGLISHMEN thing seeming unto us just , and that it might be for the profit of our religion , and of these strangers , by the tenor of these presents we have granted the same to them : yet , with the conditions hereunder written , viz. 
 That every time the said merchants of the said ship , or with any other , shall not bring such merchandise as is forbidden , and that by sufficient proof and letters testimoniali it appears that they are free from the infections of the plague , they may victuali themselves with all necessary victuals , and traffic with us , and in this Hand and dominion , and afterward may depart and follow their voyage whither they will into the Levant or else where , as all other vessels , and especially of France and other nations do , and fell and buy whatsoever merchandise they shall think good . 
 Item , that they may bring powder for cannon and harquebus , saltpeter , coal of Newcastle , plataeae of latin , tin , Steel , iron , common karsies white , course canvas to make sail for the galleys , balls of iron for shot , fine millstones , trees & masts for galleys , little and others , and in conclusion , having seen that they for the time of their abided here , did behave themselves like faithful and catholic Christians , & that his holiness hath allowed the safeconduct of the great Turke to them granted for fear of the Turkish army , and other vessels of the enemy , submitting our selves to the pleasures of his holiness , and especially because our people have occasion to pass by diverse places so far off , as England , Flanders , and all parts Westwards , and in other places , we have vouchsafed to make these our letters patents , as our faithful assistant , so as more surely , and without let they may go and return when they shall think good , with the said ship or with others at their pleasure . We therefore pray all and every of your subject's effectually that by what part soever of your jurisdiction , unto the which the said worshipful John Keel and David Filly by name abovesaid , with the ship and mariners of the said principali place or other , shall have access , sail , & 219 
 pass , and come safely with liberty without any disturbance or other impediment , that you give leave , and cause leave to be given that they may pass , stay and return , and when they please , depart , in such sort , that for our love & contentation the said worshipful John Keel , with the ship and mariners have no let , hindrance , or retention , also that you give all help and favour , a thing worthy of your justice , and to us most acceptable , to be recompensed with equal and greater service , when upon occasion it shall required . 
 And finally , we command all , and whatsoever religious people , and brother's of our religion , of whatsoever condition , degree , and state they be , and all other receivers and procurators , in all and whatsoever our priories deputed , and to be deputed by virtue of the holy obedience , and all our people , and all that are subject to the jurisdiction of our religion , that in , and by the same they hold , and repute the said worshipful John Keel in the name as abovesaid , the ship , mariners , and merchandise , without let in the same their voyage , or in any other place , that they be not molested , nor in any wise hindered , but that in all their causes and business they be of you helped , and furthered continually . In witness whereof , our seal of government is impressed to these presents in black wax . Given at Malta in our Convent , the twelfth of the month of July , in the year 1582.
