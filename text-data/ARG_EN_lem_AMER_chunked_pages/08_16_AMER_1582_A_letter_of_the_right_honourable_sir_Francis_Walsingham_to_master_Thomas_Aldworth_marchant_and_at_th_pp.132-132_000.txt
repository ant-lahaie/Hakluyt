a letter of sir Francis Walsingham to master Thomas Aidworth merchant , and at that time mayor of the ci tie of Bristol , concern their adventure in the western discovery . ft my hearty commendation , i have for certain cause defer the answer of your letter of November last till now , which i hope come all in good time . your good inclination to the western discovery i can but much commend . and for that sir Humphrey Gilbert , as you have hear long since , have be prepare into those part be ready to embark within these 10 . day , who need some further supply of ship then yet he have , i be of opinion that you shall do well if the ship or 2 . bark you write of , be put in a readiness to go alongst with he , or so soon after as you may . i hope this travel will prove profitable to the adventurer and general beneficial to the whole realm : herein i pray you confer with these bearer , m . Richard Hackluyt , and m . Thomas Steventon , to who i refer you : and so bid you hearty farewell . Richmond the 11 . of march . 1582 . your love friend , Francis Walsingham . .
