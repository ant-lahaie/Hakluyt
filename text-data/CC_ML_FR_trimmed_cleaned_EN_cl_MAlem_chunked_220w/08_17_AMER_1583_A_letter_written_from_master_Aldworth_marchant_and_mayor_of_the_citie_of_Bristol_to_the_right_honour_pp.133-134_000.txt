a letter write from . m . Thomas Aidworth merchant and mayor of the city of Bristol , to the right honourable sir Francis Walsingham principali secretary to her majesty , concern a western voyage intend for the discovery of the coast of America , lie to the southwest of Cape briton . right honourable , upon the receit of your letter direct unto i and deliver by the bearer hereof m . Richard Hakluyt and m . Steventon , bear date the 11 . of march , i present confer with my friend in private , who i know most affectionate to this godly enterprise , especial with m . William salterne deputy of our company of merchant : whereupon my self be as then sick , with as convenient speed as he can , he cause a assembly of the merchant to be gather : where after dutiful mention of your honourable disposition for the benefit of this city , he by my appointment cause your letter be direct unto i private , to be read in public , and after some good light give by m .
