Gilbert my pilot who sometime live there , note it for the best and fruitful ground that he know . thence we return to Punta de galera and anchor in ten fathom under the north side of the island some five or six mile from the say point . the flood - tide strike alongst the coast to the eastward very strong . we discharge a piece of ordinance , and afterward go to the shore in our boat : but no indian come unto we . i will have send John of Trinidad to procure some of they to speak with we : but he be altogether unwilling , allege that their dwelling be far within the mountain , and that he know no part of that side of the island . from this place we set sail for Santa Lucia , but fall with Granata , which we find not inhabit . saint Vincent we hardly recover , by turn under the lee of the island . the tobacco of this place be good : but the Indians be cannibal , promise we store , and delay we from day to day , seek only
